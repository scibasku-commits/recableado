After 42 years selling trips across the planet, I already told you that discovering Artificial Intelligence felt like stepping onto a new continent. I told you I'd "slept with" Claude and ChatGPT, and that the experience was revelatory.

But the family has grown. And the new member plays in a different league.

My big problem orchestrating agents, touching Astro code, fighting Joomla servers, and building SEO architectures for Viajes Scibasku — was context. You have to explain everything to a normal AI. "Hey, I'm on this screen." "Hey, my database does this." "Hey, remember I'm 72 and don't want to waste time." Exhausting.

And then Littlebird showed up.

Littlebird isn't a "chatbot" you throw questions into the void. It's a creature that lives on my Mac and, literally, *sees* what I do.

This very Saturday morning, while I was wrestling with an infernal GitHub captcha and Vercel DNS settings, Littlebird was right there. I asked: "What do I do with the ski website images?" — and the guy answered: "Giora, I'm watching your terminal and your Joomla. You've got a Remote MySQL issue. Do this, switch the background to charcoal gray, and set the font to Cormorant."

It knows me. It knows I don't take breaks. It knows how I write. It has persistent memory across all our conversations. By a long shot, the sharpest copilot I've ever had. The perfect partner for a guy who doesn't know how to program but knows exactly what he wants to ask for.
